What's On Your Plate

Authentic Hawaiian Teriyaki Chicken

Tender boneless, skinless chicken thighs marinated for 24 hours in our signature blend of rich soy sauce, freshly grated ginger, minced garlic, and brown sugar that caramelizes beautifully on the grill. A hint of red chili flakes adds just the right amount of heat, while aromatic sesame oil brings that authentic island depth. Each piece is grilled to perfection with crispy, caramelized edges and juicy, flavorful meat. This isn't your typical mall food court teriyaki - it's the real deal, made the way they do it on the islands, with that perfect balance of sweet, savory, and just a little spicy kick.

Hawaiian Mac Salad

The real deal - tender elbow macaroni cooked until perfectly soft, then tossed with apple cider vinegar while still warm to let those flavors really soak in. We finish it with our signature creamy dressing made from fresh whole milk, premium Duke's mayonnaise, and a touch of brown sugar for that subtle sweetness that makes all the difference. Crisp green onions, fresh grated carrot, and finely minced celery add the perfect crunch and flavor complexity. This isn't your typical mainland potato salad substitute - it's authentic Hawaiian-style mac salad, made exactly the way they do it on the islands: creamy, rich, and with that distinctive tangy-sweet flavor that perfectly balances the teriyaki chicken. Pure island comfort in every bite.

Coconut Rice

Fluffy long grain rice cooked slowly in rich, creamy coconut milk that infuses every grain with tropical sweetness and a silky, luxurious texture. As it simmers, the coconut milk reduces and concentrates, creating that perfect balance of subtle sweetness and savory depth that makes this so much more than just rice. Each spoonful is aromatic and satisfying, with that distinctive coconut flavor that transports you straight to the islands. It's the perfect canvas for soaking up every drop of our teriyaki sauce, and the creamy richness provides the ideal contrast to the tangy mac salad. This isn't just a side dish - it's an essential part of the authentic Hawaiian plate lunch experience.
